Chocolate cake is a dessert that has a chocolate flavor and chocolate frosting. The history of chocolate cake is not clear, but there are many stories about its invention. One report says that a chef in France made the first chocolate cake for Napoleon Bonaparte‘s wife, Josephine. Another story says that a woman in the United States named Sarah Lee invented chocolate cake in the 1800s.. more




  • 2 cups white sugar
  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup boiling water
  1. Prepare the dough.
  2. Activate the dry yeast by dissolving 260ml warm milk, 4 tablespoon sugar & 1 tablespoon active dry yeast.
  3. Add 1/4 tablespoon into 400grams of flour.
  4. Blend together the wet & the dry ingredients in a large bowl until we get a homogenous evenly hydrated dough.
  5. Let the dough sit & rise for 1 1/2 to 2 hours or until the dough get doubled in size.
  6. Degas the dough & divide the dough into 12 equal pieces.
  7. Roll each piece of dough into the shape of a disc which is approximately 16-18cm in diameter & not more than 2mm in thickness.
  8. Place the disc on a plastic film & repeat the same process for the next piece & stack them over the previous disc & continue the same with rest of the divided dough pieces.
  9. As butter is very soft now, we need to freeze them for 30-35minutes so that the butter will not be very soft & will be pliable & it will be easy for us to work with the dough.
  10. Roll them into long rectangular sheet.
  11. With the help of a knife or a pizza cutter, cut them into smaller triangles.
  12. Shape them into croissants and your croissant is ready!
