Tips To Make Perfect Cookies


  • Use cold butter

    • Cold butter is ideal for baked goods that should be crisp.

  • Use fresh baking powder

    • A fresh baking powder simply adds carbon dioxide to the equation, providing a more forceful pressure that encourages a dough to spread up and out.

  • Use a sift to mix your ingredients

    • Putting your flour through a sifter will break up any lumps in the flour, which means you can get a more accurate measurement.

  • Do not overwork biscuit dough

    • The more you touch the dough, the more likely the butter is to melt, and the more you mix and knead it, the tougher the biscuits will become.

  • Bake the biscuits close together

    • Placing biscuits close together on your sheet pan or skillet help your biscuits get a bit more height and flaky

  • Weigh the flour

    • The secret to better baking is weighing your ingredients—especially flour.

  • Freeze your butter

    • The latter makes it extremely easy to bake them off at a moment's notice.
