Tips To Make Perfect Cake

  • Use parchment paper rounds

    • Not only will the parchment help cookies bake more evenly, the non-stick quality also helps prevent them from cracking or breaking when lifting them off the sheet.

  • Allow to cool completely in pan

    • Assembling and/or decorating cakes before they’re completely cool is literally a recipe for disaster. The flavor hasn’t settled and the frosting will melt.

  • Keep frosting soft

    • This keeps it soft and it doesn’t crack when the cake is cut by adding a teaspoonful of vinegar to the boiled frosting

  • Do not overmix or undermix

    • Over-mixing batter, whether that’s for cakes, cupcakes, breads, muffins, etc, lends a tough-textured baked good because you’re deflating all the air and over-developing the gluten.

  • Do not open the oven

    • Don’t open the oven 25 times as the cake bakes. This lets in cool air and the drastic temperature change causes the rising cake to sink.

  • Use a whisk

    • It works much faster than sifting, and there’s less to clean up.

  • Pop those air bubbles

    • When baking a cake, rap your pans sharply on the countertop before putting them in the oven. This will eliminate most of the air bubbles.
